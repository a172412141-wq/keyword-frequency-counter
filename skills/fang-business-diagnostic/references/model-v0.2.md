# Fang 经营模型 V0.2: 阶段管理引擎与经营关系诊断

Source: `Fang经营模型_V0.2_阶段管理引擎.docx`.

## Core Thesis

Fang 经营模型 V0.2 upgrades a pure operating diagnosis model into a management model:

- Do not only judge whether a SKU is good or bad.
- First judge the current operating stage.
- Match each stage to its own KPI, red lines, expansion conditions, and stop-loss actions.
- Treat GMV as an output, not an early-stage operating goal.

The combined model is:

`经营关系诊断 x 阶段管理 x 红线控制 x 扩张条件`

## Platform Driver Judgment

Before setting priorities, identify what actually drives growth on the platform:

| Platform driver | Impact on operating judgment |
| --- | --- |
| Supply-chain stability | Determines whether orders can be carried continuously. |
| Fulfillment efficiency | Determines platform weight, user experience, and expansion ceiling. |
| Price-band fit | Determines whether the SKU enters the mainstream conversion range. |
| Campaign capacity | Determines whether the business can absorb stage-based volume. |
| Return control | Determines whether real profit is valid. |
| Cash recovery | Determines whether replenishment can roll forward. |

This is system-first operating thinking, not tactics-first operating thinking.

## Stage Map

| Stage | Main contradiction | What not to do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0-10 | Whether the model runs through | Do not chase GMV. |
| 10-30 | Whether a single SKU is stable | Do not expand SKU blindly. |
| 30-60 | Whether the model can copy to related SKU | Do not expand across supply chains. |
| 60-100 | Whether a category operating system forms | Do not rely only on a single hit SKU. |

Stage principle:

- `0-10`: prove it can make money, not that it can scale.
- `10-30`: prove it can stay stable, not that it can explode.
- `30-60`: prove it can be copied, not that it can be broadly listed.
- `60-100`: prove it can form structure, not that one point is growing.

## Stage KPI Priority

| Stage | KPI priority |
| --- | --- |
| 0-10 | Real gross profit > fulfillment runs through > returns controllable > first-order validation |
| 10-30 | Stable orders > return rate > stockout rate > per-order gross profit |
| 30-60 | Inventory turnover > related SKU replication > replenishment cycle > profit amount |
| 60-100 | Small-category scale > cash recovery > total profit > GMV |

Overall Fang philosophy:

`turnover > scale > gross profit amount > gross margin rate`

## Model Layers

| Layer | Module | Judgment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Platform base judgment | Judge whether growth is driven by ads, content, price, supply chain, fulfillment, campaign, or repurchase. |
| 2 | Stage management | Judge whether the project is in `0-10`, `10-30`, `30-60`, or `60-100`. |
| 3 | SKU operating position | Classify SKU as traffic, main, profit, or low-efficiency/abnormal. |
| 4 | Relationship diagnosis | Compare parent, product line, size, price band, inventory, ads, and profit horizontally. |
| 5 | Red lines and actions | Output actions based on profit, stockout, returns, turnover, cash flow, and expansion red lines. |

## Stage 1: 0-10 Model Validation

Purpose: verify whether a SKU has real operating value. Do not use GMV or short-term order growth as the core judgment.

Core questions:

- Are there real orders?
- Is contribution margin still positive after platform fees, logistics, return reserves, and campaign discounts?
- Can fulfillment run through?
- Are there obvious quality, description, packaging, or after-sales risks?
- Is replenishment and later expansion possible?

Core KPI:

| KPI | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Per-order real contribution margin | Whether the SKU is worth continuing. |
| First-round real orders | Whether demand exists. |
| Return/after-sales abnormality | Whether product risk is controllable. |
| Fulfillment run-through rate | Whether the supply chain is valid. |
| Replenishment cycle | Whether continuous operation is possible. |

Management actions:

- Build the full profit model.
- Complete competitor price-band, review-pain-point, and negative-review analysis.
- Control first-batch inventory; do not overstock.
- Validate with real orders, not subjective judgment.
- Establish replenishment red lines and stop-loss rules.

Forbidden actions:

- Do not use GMV as the only KPI.
- Do not list before real margin is calculated.
- Do not expand SKU during the first test round.
- Do not run negative-margin promotions just to generate orders.
- Do not declare the model valid because of a few growing orders.

## Stage 2: 10-30 Stability Validation

Purpose: verify whether a single SKU can continuously sell while returns, replenishment, profit, and fulfillment remain stable.

Core questions:

- Are orders continuous?
- Is return rate stable?
- Is there stockout risk?
- Is profit still positive after campaigns?
- Can replenishment become predictable?

Core KPI:

| KPI | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 14-day average daily sales | Whether stable sales have started. |
| Return rate | Whether real profit will be swallowed. |
| Stockout rate | Whether fulfillment and replenishment are stable. |
| Inventory coverage days | Whether turnover is healthy. |
| Post-campaign real gross profit | Whether the SKU can absorb campaigns. |

Management actions:

- Review sales, profit, returns, and inventory weekly.
- Build a replenishment calendar.
- Analyze return causes and optimize listing content, packaging, or product.
- Run limited price tests.
- Eliminate or pause replenishment for SKU that fail standards.

Forbidden actions:

- Do not expand SKU quickly because of short-term GMV growth.
- Do not ignore return rate.
- Do not replenish only after stockout.
- Do not join campaigns without profit red lines.
- Do not treat unstable SKU as main SKU.

## Stage 3: 30-60 Related SKU Replication

Purpose: copy from one successful SKU into related SKU under the same supply chain, price band, and use case.

Core questions:

- Is the main SKU stable?
- Are there 2-4 related SKU worth expanding?
- Are they still in the same supply chain?
- Will management complexity increase too much?
- Will the expansion improve turnover and total profit?

Core KPI:

| KPI | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Related SKU order share | Whether expansion is effective. |
| Main SKU inventory stability | Whether expansion hurts the core SKU. |
| Parent/product-line profit amount | Whether expansion creates real profit. |
| Turnover days | Whether inventory is becoming heavy. |
| Cash tied up | Whether expansion damages cash flow. |

Management actions:

- Expand only same-supply-chain, same-use-case, same-price-band SKU.
- Establish backup supplier A/B.
- Classify SKU into main, profit, traffic, and low-efficiency groups.
- Build parent and product-line dashboards.
- Set the expansion ban: if the main SKU is unstable, do not open a new chain.

Forbidden actions:

- Do not expand into unfamiliar categories.
- Do not expand just to increase SKU count.
- Do not let low-efficiency SKU occupy replenishment funds for A-class SKU.
- Do not keep expanding while turnover worsens.
- Do not scale a single-point SKU without supply-chain backup.

## Stage 4: 60-100 Small-Category Matrix

Purpose: form a small-category operating matrix with core SKU, profit SKU, traffic SKU, and defensive SKU. This is the first stage where scale becomes a real target, but only under controllable turnover, profit, and cash flow.

Core questions:

- Are there 3-5 stable selling SKU?
- Are at least 1-2 main SKU clear?
- Is replenishment priority clear?
- Can the business absorb platform campaigns?
- Can it handle larger inventory and deeper fulfillment complexity?

Core KPI:

| KPI | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Stable selling SKU count | Whether the business is free from one-hit dependence. |
| Parent/product-line GMV | Whether scale is forming. |
| Parent/product-line profit | Whether scale has quality. |
| Inventory turnover days | Whether expansion is healthy. |
| Cash recovery cycle | Whether replenishment is sustainable. |

Management actions:

- Build the small-category SKU matrix.
- Allocate inventory and cash by A/B/C SKU class.
- Prioritize replenishment for main SKU, control traffic SKU budget, and protect margin for profit SKU.
- Review product-line structure monthly.
- Decide whether to enter higher-level warehouse, supply-chain, or campaign investment.

Forbidden actions:

- Do not look only at GMV and ignore profit.
- Do not let inventory grow faster than profit.
- Do not let low-efficiency SKU occupy inventory long term.
- Do not scale while cash flow is unstable.
- Do not misread single-platform growth as long-term operating capability.

## Stage Entry And Stay Conditions

| Stage | Enter/stay conditions |
| --- | --- |
| 0-10 | Continuous orders have not formed; real contribution margin is unverified; return-rate data is not stable; replenishment cycle is unclear; fulfillment has not fully run through. |
| 10-30 | Real orders exist; per-order real margin is positive; listing, fulfillment, and after-sales have run through; early repeat purchase, replenishment, or continuous-sales signs exist; weekly business review is possible. |
| 30-60 | Main SKU has stable orders for 14-30 days; inventory coverage is controllable; return rate has not crossed red line; related SKU exist in the same supply chain; expansion will not hurt main-SKU replenishment funds. |
| 60-100 | At least 3 SKU sell stably; at least 1 main SKU is clear; parent or product-line profit is positive; turnover cycle is controllable; the business can absorb larger inventory, stronger campaigns, and more complex supply chain. |

## Fang Red-Line Mechanism

| Red line | Trigger and immediate action |
| --- | --- |
| Profit red line | Any SKU has negative real contribution margin for 7 consecutive days: pause replenishment immediately and recalculate price, campaign, logistics, and return cost. |
| Stockout red line | A-class SKU is expected to stock out within 7 days: pause non-core expansion and prioritize replenishment for main SKU. |
| Return red line | Return rate exceeds the internal threshold for two consecutive weeks: review product quality, packaging, images, description, and user expectation gap. |
| Inventory red line | Inventory turnover days keep worsening: ban further SKU expansion and clear low-efficiency SKU first. |
| Cash-flow red line | Replenishment funds for the next 4 weeks cannot be covered: compress non-core inventory and prioritize high-turnover, high-profit SKU. |
| Expansion red line | Main SKU model is not stable: do not enter a new supply chain, category, or price band. |

## Direct System Rules

- In any SKU, parent, product-line, or platform analysis, first judge operating stage.
- Different stages must not share one KPI system.
- `0-10` focuses on model validation, not GMV.
- `10-30` focuses on stability validation.
- `30-60` focuses on related SKU replication.
- `60-100` focuses on small-category matrix and scaled operation.
- Every expansion action must pass profit, return, stockout, inventory-turnover, and cash-flow red lines.
- If the main SKU is unstable, do not expand.
- If inventory turnover worsens, do not expand.
- If cash flow cannot cover future replenishment, do not expand.
- If real contribution margin is negative, do not continue investing because GMV is growing.

## Diagnostic Data Checklist

Ask for or extract the following when available:

- Time window and platform.
- SKU/parent/product-line structure.
- Orders and GMV by SKU.
- Per-order real contribution margin after fees, logistics, returns reserve, and discounts.
- Return rate and return reasons.
- Inventory coverage days, stockout forecast, and replenishment cycle.
- Campaign participation and post-campaign profit.
- Cash tied up and next-4-week replenishment funding gap.
- Supplier chain and whether related SKU share supply chain, use case, and price band.
